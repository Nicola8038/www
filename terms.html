Data Protection

Aktionariat AG processes data from three sources: the public data visible on the Ethereum blockchain, issuers that work with us, and our users. Blockchain-based data is distributed all over the world and outside of our control. Data you store with us is stored on dedicated hardware in Switzerland and Germany.

When using our services (e.g. our app or the widgets we provide to the issuers) to initiate blockchain-based transactions, you should be aware that these transactions will publicly and irrevocably archived on the Ethereum blockchain once you sign them and send them to the network. These transactions typically include information about how many tokens have been bought, sold, transferred or otherwise interacted with, a timestamp, and the involved addresses. They do not include personal data such as your name or email address. Nonetheless, once someone knows that a particular address belongs to you, they can connect all the transactions involving that address to you. If, for example, you are concerned with one issuer not knowing about what tokens of other issuers you own, you should use distinct address for distinct issuers.

Issuers that use our services might store personal data about you with us. This mainly concerns the shareholder registry and includes your name, address, and e-mail. You can view and manage this data through us in the Aktionariat app or alternatively get in touch with the issuer that entered your data. Note that updating your data with one issuer automatically also updates your data with other issuers. If you do not want your data to be shared between issuers, you should use separate accounts for each issuer or group of issuers whose data you wish to be kept separate. Be aware that companies are required by law to keep all information that is relevant for the shareholder registry - so if we are tasked by an issuer to keep their shareholder registry up to date, we are legally required to keep your data. Even if you are not a shareholder anymore, the law requires all data in connection with an entry in the shareholder registry to be kept for another 10 years. See also Circular 2019/01 by the Swiss Blockchain Federation.

Furthermore, there is the data you provide us with. This includes your e-mail address and notification settings once you subscribe to our newsletter or token notifications. We do not use cookies for tracking you (hence no cookie warning), but for keeping your session alive once you login to the corporate dashboard. Also, WalletConnect uses cookies to keep its sessions open when our widgets interact with your wallet through WalletConnect. Data sent to us through your usage of the registration widget or other widgets on the corporate websites of issuers is considered to be provided to us by the issuers and not by you and governed by the agreement between you and the issuer. Data you enter in the Aktionariat app, however, is governed by this agreement. You can view and edit this data at any time in the app. It includes your personal information as well as all the Ethereum addresses you have associated with your account. So far, we have no implemented an account deletion feature yet. If you wish to delete your account, please get in touch with accounts@aktionariat.com .

Communication Policy

We might send you two types of emails: our own emails and emails sent on behalf of an issuer. Our own emails include our newsletter, price updates, and other things you can subscribe to and manage here (if applicable). When installing the app, when registering through the registration widget of the issuer or when an issuer adds you to the shareholder registry, you are by default subscribed to all events of shares you hold at the 'reasonable' level. You should note that even if you disable all our notifications on this page, issuers might still use our services to deliver messages to you on behalf of them. This latter category of messages is governed by your agreements with the relevant issuer. In some cases, for example the invitation to the yearly general assembly, the issuer might be required by law to get in touch with you even if you opted out of email communications in general.
